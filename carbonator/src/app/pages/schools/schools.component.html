Carbonator in Schools

Carbonator can be used in a schools for inquiry based, blended or independent learning.

Science inquiry involves identifying and posing questions. Carbonator is a useful tool to practice constructing questions and hypotheses, and proposing possible outcomes in order to plan, conduct and reflect on scientific investigations.

  • Hypothesis testing and scenario based learning: run you scenarios to see how the planet reacts
  • Learn what a model is and how simple models like carbonator can tell us important information about the real world
  • Hands on learning around how and why greenhouse gases, aerosols and other factors affect the climate system
  • Learn how to translate physical understanding to mathematics to computer code using our simplified Carbonator model in excel
  • Export data to perform data analysis and model-observation comparisons

We encourage teachers and students to use the model for their own inquiry questions.

A good research question:

  • has multiple possible answers and should not be easily answered from, say, a web search;
  • is not biased in terminology or position;
  • is simple and manageable;
  • is focused and clear.
